TIPS FOR RELOCATING YOUR FAMILY LONG DISTANCE

So you are planning on relocating your family long distance? We all know that moving can be a bit tricky and rough. However, not all moves are made equal. Some types of moving are more complex than others. More specifically, long-distance moving. This kind of move can be hard to accomplish, however, when you add relocating your family long distance into that equation, things start to get even more serious and complicated. That’s why most people seek out the help of long distance movers NYC. Through the years of working in the moving industry, we have caught on a few tips and tricks which we are going to share with you today. Which will hopefully, in turn, help you and your family have a hassle-free move. So let’s see what we can do.

“Failing to plan is planning to fail.”

Abraham Lincoln was a man of many wise words, and none ring truer in the current situation than the one above. Relocating your family long distances is not a thing you do overnight. It’s something that requires a well-thought-out plan and great communication skills between you, your partner and your little ones. So what are the things you need to think so long and hard about? Well, here are a few recommendations:

  • What are you going to do with all your belongings? Are you going to declutter? Or maybe hire Fort Lee movers and put them in storage? What are the belongings you absolutely cannot live without out?
  • Do you have kids? Are you planning to? You might want to consider the impact that the new relocation will have on their friends, school, etc.
  • How are you going to approach this move? Are you going to hire professional movers or try a DIY move?

The number one thing people get wrong when moving long distances is not packing on time. If you are already packing in a hurry there are ways to do it more efficiently. However, whatever the time frame of your packing is, we recommend that you bundle up all the belongings you rarely use first. Oh, and this is an awesome opportunity to get you, kids, on helping you pack, making them a part of relocation as well.

Everyone should pack their own belongings

After the move is done, sit back, relax and enjoy a slice of pizza!

A good idea when packing is to ask each and every family member to get a moving box with their name on it. In which they will pack their most essential belongings – stuff like your electronic devices, planners, medical documentation. Anything and everything you might need to be available when you arrive in your new home. Given that unpacking, your belongings take time, having your own personal box of items you might need when you arrive at your new location is a must-have.

However, moving can be as we said, quite a stressful experience. That’s why we recommend that you hire movers. So they can help you with all the heavy lifting and you can be left to enjoy quality time with your family.
